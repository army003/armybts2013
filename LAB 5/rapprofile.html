RM, Moonchild



RM was born on September 12, 1994 in Ilsan, South Korea.
Nam Jun has an IQ of 148 and is ranked in the TOP 1.3% of national high school mock exams in language, math, foreign language and social studies. RM is also fluent in English, which he studied with his mother as a child and through friends. He called himself an atheist and earned the name Brain Monster. Prior to his debut with BTS, he was an underground rapper under the stage name "Runch Randa". He has released several tracks and once collaborated with fellow underground rapper Zico. He was part of an underground Korean hip-hop group called "Daenamhyup". The group was active from 2009 to 2013, although RM has not been involved in recent years to focus on BTS.

Agust D(SUGA), Agust D


Min Yoongi was born on March 9, 1993 in Daegu, South Korea. He was the youngest child in the family and attended Daejeon Elementary School, Kwanyum High School and Apgujeong High School. At the age of 13, he began writing songs and learning MIDI. He worked part-time in a recording studio when he was 17. From then on, Yoongi began making and arranging music, rapping and practicing. Prior to joining Big Hit Entertainment, he was known as the underground rapper Gloss. As part of the hip-hop team D-Town, in 2010 he released the track "518-062" in commemoration of the Gwangju uprising.He made his debut as a solo artist with the release of the Agust D mixtape under the stage name of the same name on August 15, 2016.

J-Hope, Daydream



Jung Hoseok was born on February 18, 1994 in Gwangju, South Korea, where he lived with his parents and older sister. Prior to debuting with BTS, he was part of the Neuron dance team. Hoseok was quite famous for his dancing skills long before he became a professional idol; he won local competitions and won the national dance competition in 2008. On June 13, 2013, Hoseok debuted as a boy group member BTS with the single "No More Dream" from his debut single album 2 Cool 4 Skool. On March 1, 2018, J-Hope released his first mixtape, Hope World, along with the single "Daydream". The video for the B-side single "Airplane" was released on March 6th. Hope World debuted at # 63 and then climbed to # 38 on the prestigious US Album Chart Billboard 200, making J-Hope the only Korean solo artist ever to reach such a high position on the chart.
